ForwardJS is a conference for the passionate community of Javascript; it's a 1 day event that's normally held twice a year, once in San Francisco, and another one in Ottawa.
On Tuesday I had the opportunity to volunteer and spend the entire day at the ForwardJS conference held here in San Francisco. It was a collection of some great people in the world of Javascript. And as someone who is used to mainly going to Ad & FinTech conferences it was a refreshing shift to an event where the collective group was extremely engaged and welcoming from a networking & non-sales approach type of mindset. Being this was my first time attending, I openly admit that my sample size is limited, but overall I thought the content was helpful and that the community seems to really support inclusiveness & the growth of new developers, like myself, to sharpening their skills and figuring out how to advance both their work & side projects. I'd recommend the conference to anyone who is looking to learn better practices, network with people to collaborate on projects, or hear about interesting projects that some of the top companies are working on internally. The conference had a little of it all and videos of certain talks will be posted soon on the Lectures Page! Additionally, for those of you that are looking for a deeper dive into a specific discipline, they offer online courses & 1 day workshops on the front & tail end of the conference days.
As a bootcamp graduate I find these types of events invaluable due to the nature of how much of the unknown unknowns they help me to discover. Just by attending the event I was able to have dozens of conversations with people who are going, have gone, or enjoy providing advice on going through the initial ramp-up learning process for becoming an active developer. I had a nice conversation with a woman on the pro & cons of server-side rendering and why it's not always the best approach depending on the size of your application. While this is something that I had been told in the past, the beginning of my development career has been like drinking from 2 fire hoses so it had escaped me until this random chat that occurred waiting for the next speaker to start. I was then able to find some helpful articles like this. that helped me to get a better grasp on the core concepts that I'm sure will be valuable in either an interview setting or during an upcoming project.
I'll definitely be keeping my eyes open for more events like ForwardJS in the future as I continue my journey. Also, for those of you like me who don't have an employer sponsoring conferences, I was able to secure a free ticket by offering to volunteer for the event by cold-emailing the conference info@ email. They needed help and by showing up a couple hours early and checking people in to get their badges I got to attend for free. And the best part was that check-in wrapped up shortly after the keynote ended so I got to attend all of the track timeslots for the entire day.
